HUBEDA

Workshop on Human Behavior Data Acquisition for Human-Robot Interaction

Co-Located with IEEE ICRA 2025
Workshop Date: TBD
Workshop Venue: Atlanta, Georgia, US

Call for Papers

A vast number of advancements have been made in making robots engage in more natural human-robot interaction (HRI). To close the gap on enabling safe seamless human-aware HRI, collection of large data on human behavior, specially diverse multi-person interactions spanning large participant counts, is still an open challenge. Data on human behavior spans visual information on human movements, gestures, and interactions, spoken content, textual information, and physiological data such as heart rate, electromyography, and electrodermal activity.

Through keynote talks from invited papers, paper and demo submissions, and dialogue via lightning talks, poster sessions, collaborative group deliberations, and a panel session, HUBEDA addresses fundamental questions on the acquisition, analysis, and use of large-scale data on human behavior and interactions to enable data-informed HRI. Following topics are of interest.

  • Datasets on single-person behavior and multi-person interaction for HRI
  • Multimodal setups integrating visual, audio, inertial, tactile, and physiological sensing for full-range capture
  • Augmented reality / virtual reality (AR/VR) approaches to scale up human subject data collection for HRI
  • AI, machine learning (ML), vision, and natural language processing (NLP) approaches to estimate parameters of interest for HRI from datasets on human behavior
  • Leveraging large vision and language models to decipher human activity, text, and spoken content
  • Robotic implementations driven using human behavior datasets
  • Robot learning from human behavior datasets and multi-person interactions
  • Applications involving multiple agents such as collaborative assembly, handover, repair
  • Ethical practices in data collection for single/multi-person behavior
  • Addressing challenges in reaching out to diverse population groups, e.g., children, older adults, and individuals with disabilities, to scale up data collection

HUBEDA is supported through a generous donation from the De Luca Foundation.

CALL FOR PAPERS

We invite authors to submit papers of up to 6 pages (including references) on the topics of interest. Accepted papers will be published on the workshop webpage and on arXiv. Authors are also invited to submit extended abstracts of up to 4 pages (including references) to be presented at the workshop on early-phase work, late-breaking results, and demonstrations, to be presented at the workshop. Authors of accepted papers will be invited to submit a full paper a special issue (to be determined) to enable publications associated with the workshop. Papers proposing a demo at the workshop will be expected to provide a live demonstration at the venue.

The goal of the workshop is to bootstrap research on data-driven methods in HRI. A working result on a robot is not strictly necessary for acceptance of papers. Rather, papers must demonstrate how their setup, methods, algorithms, and/or datasets provide human behavior data to HRI and/or robotics in general. For demos, though working demos with a real robot are encouraged, demos with a synthetic prototype will also be acceptable so long as the relevance to the topics of interest to HUBEDA is evident.

Submission link: https://easychair.org/my/conference?conf=hubeda2025

Papers submissions should follow the template at https://2025.ieee-icra.org/announcements/paper-submissions/.
